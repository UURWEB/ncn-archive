Two Names, One Confederation

The Northern Confederation of Noble
The earlier and smaller state centered on Noble, Noblecrest, and neighboring northern territories. Its name emphasized Noble’s leadership and the original confederate compact.

Expansion Beyond Noble
As additional northern states, occupied districts, and allied administrations entered the system, the old title no longer described the territory accurately.

The Northern Confederate Nations
At its greatest territorial size, the government adopted the plural title Northern Confederate Nations. The change presented the NCN as a union of several northern nations rather than an enlarged Noble state.

Contraction and Dissolution
War, administrative fragmentation, and the collapse of confederate authority reduced the NCN. Later remnant organizations reused both names, sometimes to claim different kinds of legitimacy.

Archivist’s note: Modern records should distinguish the historical state from later militant remnant groups. The former NCN government did not survive as a continuous recognized authority.

Several final-period documents were deliberately removed from the public index. Recovery staff found references to a distributed “continuity archive” hosted across civilian systems.
