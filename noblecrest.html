The City at the Center

Noblecrest grew from the administrative town of the old Kingdom of Noble into the capital of a northern confederation. When the state expanded beyond Noble itself, the city became the seat of the renamed Northern Confederate Nations.

Civic Square

The Parliament Hall, Ministry of Records, Confederate Treasury, and memorial colonnade surrounded the square. Public ceremonies were broadcast from a balcony above the western entrance.

Grand Northern Station

The station linked Noblecrest to the northern provinces and became the principal evacuation hub during the final emergency. Platform maps show a sealed service corridor that does not appear on public plans.

Broadcast House

NCN Radio transmitted news, weather, and parliamentary sessions. The last confirmed broadcast ended with twelve seconds of carrier tone and a whispered reference to “the white corridor.”

Crest Hill

A wooded rise above the government district containing old signal towers, civil-defense tunnels, and the national memorial. A later U.U.R. survey recorded low-power electrical activity beneath the hill.
